AquaSentry
Smart, low-cost, local water quality sentinel
A self-powered device that measures water quality at handpumps and taps, shows a clear verdict for everyday users, and stores precise readings for technicians. Designed for rural deployment with minimal maintenance.

Problem — Overview
Unseen contamination at source
Millions rely on untested handpumps and taps. Contaminated water causes disease, costs, and lost productivity.
Waterborne Diseases
Cholera, Typhoid, Diarrhea
Hidden Toxins
Fluoride, Arsenic, Heavy metals
Low testing
Expensive labs, rare checks
High impact
Children and farmers most affected
Problem — Root Causes
Why monitoring is missing
High equipment cost, lack of power, no local expertise, and sensor maintenance gaps create blind spots. AquaSentry targets these weaknesses with affordability, battery autonomy, and simple UI.
Solution — Overview
AquaSentry — Tap/Handpump Guardian
Solar/battery-powered sensor node with simple color-coded verdicts and local data logging for maintenance teams.
  • Local verdict: Drinkable / Washing only / Unsafe
  • Compact: Low-cost build (~₹3.5k)
  • Logs: Hourly entries on microSD, quick Prev/Next and Prev-Day buttons
Design: verdict + icon (futuristic UI)
How it works — Hardware
Sensors, Power, Enclosure
TDS (or pH) probe in flow, Arduino Nano/ESP32, 18650 battery with TP4056 charging, MT3608 boost, small OLED, and microSD logger. Optional mini solar panel and DS3231 RTC for hourly logs.
How it works — Software
Logic & Display
Sensors → average readings → verdict thresholds → display (icon + one-line text) → log CSV to microSD. Buttons: Prev / Next / Prev-Day / Live.
Impact & Roadmap
Deploy, Test, Scale
Pilot in 2 villages, refine thresholds and casing, build training for local maintenance, then scale via local artisans under Atmanirbhar Bharat.
Next steps: prototype demo, user testing, grant/seed funding, community workshops.
